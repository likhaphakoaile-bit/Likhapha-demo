Basotho Crafts and Art

The Basotho have a rich traditional culture visible in their beautiful crafts. Visitors can see these hand-made items in villages, towns, and major centers like Maseru and Leribe. Explore the artistry below.

Woven Basket: Handmade baskets perfect for storing blankets, vegetables, or fruits. Made from trees or various grasses.

Thal-shaped Hat: Made of grass called mosea, sometimes with lehloli. The conical Basotho hat is decorated with a small knob resembling Mount Qiloane next to Thaba-Bosiu.

Pottery: Made from clay and minerals, shaped and fired. Used for storing or serving water and traditional foods like motoho, mabele, and lihoapa.

Beadwork: Necklaces, bracelets, and decorative items made of clay or plastic beads. Worn during traditional events.
