Traditional Basotho Music & Dance

Music and dance are an integral part of Basotho culture, expressing moods and traditions. Instruments like Lesiba for music and dances like Litolobonya showcase the unique rhythms and sounds of the Basotho people.

Traditional Dances

Mokhibo

A dance performed primarily by women and girls, characterized by graceful shoulder and neck movements while kneeling.

Mohobelo

An energetic men's dance featuring stomping and leaping movements.

Litolobonya

A women's dance performed exclusively with accompanying songs.

Liphotha

A traditional Basotho dance associated with Lesotho and surrounding areas.

Selialia

A courtship dance where young girls choose partners through rhythmic, charming movements.

Lipina tsa Mokopu

A cultural dance designed to teach young people about Basotho traditions and values.

Selete

An energetic men's dance with quick footwork symbolizing strength and bravery.

Maqekha

Performed during girls' initiation rituals, ceremonial and connected to ancestral traditions.

Focho

Combines foot stomping with music from Lesiba, performed to entertain, court, or compete.

Traditional Instruments

Lesiba

A string instrument played by lightly plucking or sucking the string, producing melodic tones for herdsmen in the fields.

mamokhorong

Made from a can, a stalk, and wire, played by holding on the head or waist, creating unique sounds.

Moropa

An African drum used during ceremonies to create a resonant communal rhythm.

Thomo

A bow instrument with a calabash resonator, string made of horse hair, used in traditional music.

Lekokulo

A flute traditionally played by herd boys.

khabulubulu

it is made up of a string tied up tied to a curved stick it is makes a low vibrating sound.

lekhitla la lenaka

intrument made from animal horn it makes a deep and loud sound it is used to call people or used when there is danger in the community or to call people on gatherings.

lekhitla la lesapo

it is a basotho traditional instrument made from animal bone it is blown to make high pitched sounds by herdboys on ceremonies.

lekolulo

basotho musical instrument made of lehlaka or wood it is blown with the mouth to make music it is mostly played by herdboys while looking after animals.

sekebeku sa lehlaka

basotho instrument made of dry grass it is also used to make a rhythimic sound when singing or dancing as to keep the beat.

sekebeku sa tsepe

is a basotho traditional instrument made from a metal one shake it or hit it as to make rhythimic sound it is used when singing or dancing as to keep the beat.

sekupu

an instrument used to produce sound when making some dances such as litolobonya or mokhibo.
